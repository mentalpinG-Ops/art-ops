Analysis, move by move.

art-ops describes the analysis of cultural artefacts as operations — single, nameable moves on material, each with a declared profile — so that analyses can be planned mechanically, moves can be combined across traditions, and every step's reliability is checked item by item.

The idea in three sentences
1

Analytical traditions contradict each other in their justifications — but many of their moves are identical or combinable. art-ops works at the level of the move.

2

Every operation is a profile: what it needs from the material, what it demands of the analyst, what it produces, what it forbids. Analysis sequences are planned mechanically from these profiles — including the conflicts between operations.

3

Honesty is a construction principle: the register distinguishes empirically tested statements from reading-derived hypotheses, and every generated plan declares this line by line.

What is actually tested — as of today

Most of the register above is still hypothesis. A conflict is the register's term for a rule saying two operations can't run in the same analytical pass — most such rules are still untested reading. Four material findings stand. The most notable one is not a success story — a declared conflict was put to a test and did not survive it. The underlying test documentation is internal and not published with this repo — the ledger cites it; it cannot yet be independently inspected.

What it cannot do — yet

The limits, laid open

register

Mostly hypothesis. Profiles and conflicts are derived from source wording, not tested against material — see the evidence ledger above.

the planner

Plans; does not execute. A run mode is deliberately deferred until the register is more broadly validated.

interpretive ops

Have no hard falsifier. Their outputs are justified readings with a mandatory counter-reading, not measurements.

modality

Sound, moving image, numerical data and code are not covered. Image citation rests on an untested region convention (RCC).
